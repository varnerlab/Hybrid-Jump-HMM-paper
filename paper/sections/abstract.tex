% abstract.tex

Per-asset generators for synthetic financial returns, such as the
hidden Markov models with explicit jump components used in the
\texttt{JumpHMM.jl} package, preserve marginal structure but say
nothing about cross-sectional factor dependence. The standard fix
is to compose each generator draw with a single-index market
factor, but the naive recipe inflates the marginal variance
quadratically in the factor loading, while replacing the residual
with a Gaussian draw recovers the loading exactly but discards
the marginal entirely. We derived a closed-form variance
correction that sat between these failure modes, rescaling the
idiosyncratic draw by a scalar chosen so the composed series
inherited the generator's marginal variance while recovering the
calibrated factor loading. A sign-preserving clip and an
$R^2$-driven branch for index-tracking assets handled the
remaining edge cases. Evaluated on a $424$-ticker US equity and ETF
universe against the SPY market path with \texttt{JumpHMM.jl}
marginals, the hybrid construction recovered the factor loading to
a median absolute error of $0.018$ and passed a per-ticker
Kolmogorov--Smirnov test against the real marginal at rate $50\%$,
versus $4\%$ for naive composition and below $1\%$ for the
Gaussian-residual baseline. Three alternative generators fit
directly to OLS residuals (JumpHMM-on-residuals, Politis--Romano
block bootstrap, GARCH(1,1)-$t$) achieved higher KS pass rates of
$67$--$76\%$; on a per-ticker one-day $99\%$ Value-at-Risk
backtest, hybrid and residual-fit JumpHMM were statistically
indistinguishable (mean exceedance $0.95\%$ and $0.99\%$ against
the nominal $1\%$, Kupiec pass rates $80\%$ and $83\%$). Hybrid
was therefore preferred when the per-asset generator was specified
in full-return units; the residual-fit alternatives were stronger
on marginal body fidelity when the workflow permitted a dedicated
residual generator.
